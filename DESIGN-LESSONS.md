# SlapMania — Design lessons (distilled from playtest feedback, 2026-07-11)

The owner's feedback wave, distilled into principles + a concrete backlog.
Every future campaign/world/boss gets checked against this list.

## Principles

1. **A world is a PLACE, not a palette.** Retinting the farm is not a dojo.
   Each world needs its own architecture at human scale: the dojo is a BUILDING
   you fight inside (huge, but roofed, with interior walls/pillars); the tech
   campus looks like a real hi-tech campus; the therapy room IS a room —
   gigantic couch, polished wood, plush leather, steampunk-expensive, a giant
   cat watching, Jung's Red Book, persona/shadow imagery. The player should be
   able to say where they are with the HUD off.
2. **Rosters are curated per world.** No global volunteer pool. Every world
   gets a targeted list of who makes sense there (Ava in the desert is wrong).
   Volunteers wear the place: desert = shirts/shawls/turbans, male AND female,
   suited to the environment. Curation is validated by an agent pass.
3. **Bosses need a payoff loop.** A boss that just gets slapped isn't a boss
   (Tick-Tock Tom lesson). A boss must: talk trash, telegraph its gimmick
   visually, TRANSFORM or react as you progress, and end with a dramatic
   payoff + a story punchline. Tick-Tock v2: bounces in place declaring itself
   UNSLAPPABLE, absorbs cumulative points (mass/height proxy), then at the
   threshold dramatically blows away. Charlie: "..." → "And as we can see,
   everything can in fact be slapped."
4. **The mechanic IS the story beat.** Quicksand sinking (slap Don's man out of
   quicksand before he sinks — falling hitbox, shorter slappers advantaged, the
   player CHOOSES who fits the job), the escape run, the shot clock — each new
   mechanic should be the punchline of its scene, not decoration.
5. **Slapper choice should matter per challenge.** At least some challenges
   should pose "who do you bring?" (quicksand favors short; tonnage favors
   power). An unpinned challenge that never says why you'd pick differently is
   a missed design.
6. **Correspondence is sacred.** Whatever touches, reacts. Open palm = slap.
   Closed fist passing through a face must NOT be a ghost — it should thud as
   a discounted body blow / illegal punch, with referee disapproval. (P exists
   to open the palm; the fist still exists physically.)
7. **The reward ladder belongs to the arena.** Milestones (barricade, moo,
   duel, hay wall) are farm-flavored; each world should re-flavor them, and
   the ~50m "blockage" (landing-zone solids/hay wall) shouldn't cap most
   worlds — replace with per-world variation, incl. creatures that can
   influence a flight.
8. **Cutscenes own the screen.** No HUD, no cards, nothing over the cast
   (CHALLENGE CLEARED sat on Charlie's close-up — fixed via body.cine .card).
9. **Commentary knows who flew.** Pronouns follow the volunteer; lines rotate
   so repeats don't repeat. Applies to ALL flavor text going forward.
10. **Movement gimmicks must keep the exam winnable.** Range of motion tuned
    so the graded target (face) is genuinely reachable each cycle (Pogo hop
    lowered 0.55→0.40, period 0.9→1.05). If a validator can't clear it, it
    doesn't ship.
11. **Comedy is warm, parody is archetypal.** Desert oil storyline etc. go
    through a comedy+geopolitics validation pass: punch at situations and
    institutions, never at ethnicities; outfits are affectionate costume, not
    caricature. Names always parody (Tremendous Don precedent).
12. **Every fair is SOMEBODY's story.** Campaign star with a real arc per
    world: Skellington-parody star for the Haunted Fair; oil-prospecting arc
    for the desert; gym-bro arc (weightlifters, powerlifter boss) instead of a
    yoga campaign; Don's arc = he wants to bulldoze the kids' playground for a
    billion-dollar UN HQ ("No, not our playground!").
